Crisis Timeline

Today — Hour 0
Iran's Oil Ministry announces complete suspension of crude exports
State television broadcasts statement from Oil Minister citing "economic warfare" by Western powers and pledging exports will remain halted until all nuclear-related sanctions are lifted unconditionally.
+3 Hours
Brent crude spikes 14% on ICE exchange; circuit breakers triggered
Energy markets in London and New York recorded the sharpest intraday swing in crude prices since the COVID-19 demand collapse of 2020. Trading was briefly halted twice on the ICE exchange.
+5 Hours
Pentagon deploys USS Abraham Lincoln battle group to Persian Gulf
Defense Department officials confirmed the repositioning of the carrier strike group as a "precautionary and deterrent measure," denying that military action was imminent.
+7 Hours
OPEC+ calls emergency virtual summit for Monday morning
The cartel's secretariat issued a statement saying all member nations would be represented at an extraordinary meeting to discuss collective responses to the market disruption.
+9 Hours
EU activates Energy Emergency Framework; Germany declares "energy crisis"
Chancellor Weber invoked emergency energy powers, ordering industrial consumers to reduce gas consumption and fast-tracking approval of two new LNG import terminals.
+12 Hours
IAEA: Iran has suspended inspector access to Natanz facility
The announcement significantly darkened the diplomatic atmosphere, with Western officials describing it as a deliberate escalation designed to maximize leverage in any future negotiations.
+18 Hours
UN Security Council meets; Russian and Chinese vetoes block resolution
A U.S.-drafted resolution calling for Iran to immediately resume exports and re-admit IAEA inspectors was blocked by both permanent members. Moscow cited "disproportionate Western pressure."
+24 Hours
Iran's IRGC Navy conducts live-fire exercise in Strait of Hormuz
The exercises, which included fast-boat swarm tactics and anti-ship missile launches, were widely interpreted as a direct warning to the U.S. carrier group and Gulf shipping lanes.
+36 Hours
Saudi Arabia and UAE announce coordinated 2M bbl/day output increase
The announcement provided temporary relief to energy markets, but analysts cautioned that neither country could sustain such levels long-term and could not fully replace Iranian volumes.
+48 Hours
G7 foreign ministers demand Iran resume exports; new sanctions threatened
A joint communiqué warned Tehran that the group was prepared to implement an expanded sanctions regime targeting Iran's aviation sector, petrochemicals industry, and third-party financial intermediaries.
+72 Hours — NOW
Diplomatic back-channel talks reported in Oman; no breakthrough
Sources familiar with the matter say intermediaries from Oman and Qatar have been shuttling between Tehran and Washington, but fundamental gaps remain over nuclear enrichment and sanctions sequencing.

Editorial Standards

All reporting published by Global Dispatch adheres to the following standards:

  • Every factual claim is verified by a minimum of two independent sources before publication.
  • Analysis and commentary are clearly labelled and separated from news reporting.
